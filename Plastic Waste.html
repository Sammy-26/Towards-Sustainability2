About 4 Bins

Smart Compacting Bins: Uses an automated, internal compression mechanism powered by solar energy or direct electricity to maximize volume and reduce collection frequency.

  • System Name: Automated Volume Reduction System (AVRS)
  • How it Works: Built-in sensors detect fill levels, triggering an internal mechanical press that crushes waste, allowing the bin to hold up to 5x more trash before needing to be emptied.
  • Key Detail: Significantly lowers operational costs and carbon emissions by reducing the number of garbage truck collection trips needed.


AI Sorting Bins: Uses computer vision and deep learning algorithms to automatically identify, categorize, and separate waste at the exact point of disposal.

  • System Name: Intelligent Waste Recognition System (IWRS)
  • How it Works: Multiple cameras and sensors scan the disposed object, and an automated internal mechanism directs it into the correct recycling or trash compartment.
  • Key Detail: Eliminates human sorting error and prevents cross-contamination in recycling streams before waste even reaches a sorting facility.


IOT Smart Self-Driving Bins: Uses autonomous navigation, obstacle detection, and cloud connectivity to move independently to designated collection points or docking stations.

  • System Name: Autonomous Waste Rover System (AWRS).
  • How it Works: Leverages LiDAR, ultrasonic sensors, and IoT networks to navigate public spaces, moving to high-traffic areas when needed and returning to a base station when full.
  • Key Detail: Removes the physical labor of dragging heavy bins to the curb and optimizes municipal collection by gathering themselves at a single, centralized pickup zone.


Monitoring Bins: Uses a network of smart telemetry sensors attached to standard bins to track capacity, temperature, and collection metrics in real-time.

  • System Name: Real-Time Waste Telemetry System (RWTS).
  • How it Works: Ultrasonic fill-level sensors transmit live data to a centralized cloud dashboard, alerting sanitation teams exactly when a bin requires servicing.
  • Key Detail: Prevents unsanitary overfills and allows cities to switch from rigid, calendar-based pickup schedules to highly efficient, dynamic, demand-driven routes.
